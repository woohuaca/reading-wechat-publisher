# WeChat Editorial Checklist

Run this after the first draft is structurally complete.

## Opening

- Does the opening present a real problem in the first 3 to 5 paragraphs?
- Is there one sentence that tells the reader why this article matters now?
- Can a distracted reader understand the article angle without reading the whole piece?

## Structure

- Does each section title carry meaning instead of acting as a generic label?
- Is there a single through-line from opening to closing?
- Are any two neighboring sections saying almost the same thing?

## Mobile Readability

- Are paragraphs short enough for phone screens?
- Are there too many dense lists in a row?
- Is there enough rhythm between argument, example, and takeaway?

## Visuals

- Does each image reduce cognitive load or improve pacing?
- Is there at least one image that explains the central idea, not just decorates it?
- Are image paths local where possible?

## Ending

- Does the article end on a memorable line rather than a note or disclaimer?
- Does the closing reinforce the core phrase or core tension from the book?
